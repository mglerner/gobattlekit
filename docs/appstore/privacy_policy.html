GoBattleKit Privacy Policy

Last updated 23 June 2026

GoBattleKit is a companion app for Pokémon GO PvP. This policy explains what the app does with your information. The short version is that the app keeps your data on your device and does not send it anywhere.

What the app collects

Nothing about you is collected. GoBattleKit has no account, no sign-in, and no user profile. It does not ask for your name, email, location, or any other personal detail.

Data you bring into the app

You can import a CSV of your Pokémon that you export from a separate app called PokeGenie, and you can save your own IV targets. Both are written only to GoBattleKit's storage on your device. They are never uploaded, shared, or sent to the developer or anyone else. Deleting the app removes them.

Network use

When it runs, GoBattleKit downloads public Pokémon game data and PvP rankings from the PvPoke project over a secure connection. These downloads only bring data into the app. Nothing about you or your Pokémon is sent out.

Analytics, advertising, and tracking

There are none. The app contains no analytics service, no advertising, and no tracking technology. It does not use an advertising identifier.

Children

GoBattleKit does not collect data from anyone, including children, and shows no user-generated content.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new date at the top.
